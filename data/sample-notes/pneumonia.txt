Chief Complaint: Fever, cough, and productive sputum.

History of Present Illness:
A 47-year-old female with no significant past medical history presents with a 5-day history of fever, chills, and productive cough. She notes pleuritic chest pain on inspiration. She denies recent travel or known TB exposure.

Physical Exam:
Temp 101.8F, HR 108, RR 24, BP 118/72, SpO2 92% on room air. Auscultation reveals crackles at the right lower lung field.

Diagnostics:
CXR shows right lower lobe consolidation. WBC 15.6K with left shift. Procalcitonin elevated.

Assessment:
- Community acquired pneumonia (right lower lobe)
- Hypoxia

Plan:
Admit for IV antibiotics (ceftriaxone + azithromycin), supplemental oxygen targeting SpO2 >94%, sputum and blood cultures, repeat CXR in 48 hours.
