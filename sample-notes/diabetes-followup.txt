Chief Complaint: Routine diabetes follow-up.

History of Present Illness:
A 55-year-old woman with longstanding type 2 diabetes mellitus presents for routine follow-up. She reports adherence to metformin 1000 mg BID and semaglutide weekly. Home glucose readings average 140-160 mg/dL. She denies hypoglycemic episodes. She reports tingling in both feet worsening over the past 3 months.

Physical Exam:
BP 132/78, BMI 31 kg/m². Sensation diminished to monofilament testing in bilateral feet. No foot ulcers or skin breakdown.

Labs:
HbA1c 7.8% (prior 7.2%). Microalbumin/creatinine ratio 45 mg/g. LDL 118 mg/dL.

Assessment:
- Type 2 diabetes mellitus with suboptimal control
- Diabetic peripheral neuropathy
- Early diabetic nephropathy (microalbuminuria)

Plan:
Intensify lifestyle interventions; increase semaglutide to 1 mg weekly.
Initiate gabapentin 300 mg nightly for neuropathy.
Start lisinopril 10 mg daily for renal protection.
Refer to podiatry for regular foot care.
